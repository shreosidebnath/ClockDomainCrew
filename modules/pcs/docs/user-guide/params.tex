\section{Parameter Descriptions}

The hardware parameters for the \textbf{Pcs} core are described in Table~\ref{table:params}. These parameters control the 64b/66b encoding, scrambling, and synchronization state machines required for 10GBASE-R operation.

\renewcommand*{\arraystretch}{1.4}
\begin{longtable}[H]{
    | p{0.22\textwidth}
    | p{0.10\textwidth}
    | p{0.08\textwidth}
    | p{0.08\textwidth}
    | p{0.42\textwidth} |
  }
  \hline
  \textbf{Name} & \textbf{Type} & \textbf{Min} & \textbf{Max} & \textbf{Description} \\ \hline \hline
  \endfirsthead
  \hline
  \textbf{Name} & \textbf{Type} & \textbf{Min} & \textbf{Max} & \textbf{Description} \\ \hline \hline
  \endhead

  % --- Bus Arch ---
  dataW         & Int     & 32      & 64      & Internal data bus width (32 or 64 bits) \\ \hline
  ctrlW         & Int     & 4       & 8       & XGMII control bus width (dataW / 8) \\ \hline
  hdrW          & Int     & 2       & 2       & 64b/66b sync header width \\ \hline

  % --- Interface Enables ---
  txGbxIfEn     & Boolean & false   & true    & Enable gearbox handshaking for TX path \\ \hline
  rxGbxIfEn     & Boolean & false   & true    & Enable gearbox handshaking for RX path \\ \hline
  bitReverse    & Boolean & false   & true    & Reverse bit order for SERDES compatibility \\ \hline

  % --- Diagnostic/Test ---
  scramblerDisable & Boolean & false & true  & Disable $x^{58} + x^{39} + 1$ scrambling logic \\ \hline
  prbs31En      & Boolean & false   & true    & Enable hardware PRBS31 pattern logic \\ \hline

  % --- Pipelining ---
  txSerdesPipeline & Int  & 0       & --      & Pipeline stages for TX SERDES output \\ \hline
  rxSerdesPipeline & Int  & 0       & --      & Pipeline stages for RX SERDES input \\ \hline

  % --- Link Alignment (Bitslip) ---
  bitslipHighCycles & Int & 0       & --      & Cycles to hold \textit{serdesRxBitslip} high \\ \hline
  bitslipLowCycles  & Int & 0       & --      & Cooldown cycles between bitslip pulses \\ \hline

  % --- BER Monitoring ---
  count125Us    & Double  & --      & --      & Cycles representing a 125$\mu$s window for Bit Error Rate monitoring \\ \hline

  \caption{PCS Parameter Descriptions}\label{table:params}
\end{longtable}

\subsection{Example Instantiation}

The \textbf{Pcs} core is typically instantiated between the MAC and the high-speed Transceivers (SERDES). An example configuration using a 64-bit data path is shown below:

\begin{lstlisting}[language=Scala]
  // Define PCS configuration
  val myPcsParams = PcsParams(
    dataW = 64,
    ctrlW = 8,
    bitReverse = true,
    prbs31En = false
  )

  // Instantiate the PCS core
  val ethernetPcs = Module(new Pcs(myPcsParams))
  
  // Connect clocks (TX and RX are usually asynchronous)
  ethernetPcs.io.txClk := macClock
  ethernetPcs.io.rxClk := serdesRxClock
\end{lstlisting}
